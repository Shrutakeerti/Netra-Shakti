Cataracts: Understanding the Condition

Explore the causes, symptoms, treatments, and preventative measures for one of the leading causes of vision impairment globally.

Symptoms of Cataracts

Cataracts can develop slowly and may not affect your vision initially. However, over time, they can lead to several visual impairments.

  • Blurred or cloudy vision
  • Double vision in one eye
  • Sensitivity to light and glare
  • Difficulty seeing at night

Causes of Cataracts

Various factors can contribute to the development of cataracts, including aging and environmental factors.

  • Aging
  • Diabetes and other health conditions
  • Excessive exposure to sunlight
  • Use of corticosteroid medications

Treatment Options for Cataracts

Early symptoms may be improved with new glasses, brighter lighting, anti-glare sunglasses, or magnifying lenses. If these measures do not help, surgery is the only effective treatment.

  • Cataract surgery (lens replacement)
  • Prescription eyeglasses
  • Lifestyle and dietary adjustments

Preventing Cataracts

Though not all cataracts can be prevented, some strategies can help reduce your risk of developing them.

  • Regular comprehensive eye exams
  • Wearing UV-protective sunglasses
  • Maintaining a healthy diet rich in antioxidants
  • Avoiding smoking and excessive alcohol
